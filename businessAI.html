Project Introduction and Data Analysis

I developed a model in TensorFlow reaching over 90% validation accuracy that predicts whether a given employee will leave their job. The training data collected on employees has a sample size of 1129 with each sample having 12 features. These features are broken into two categories as shown below.

The first category, named "Concrete Data" in the above, holds objective information on the employee, and the second category holds absract personality scores that the employees self report. For these data, the employee is asked to rate themselves on a scale of 1-10 on how strongly the qualities above describe them, with 1 being weak agreement and 10 being strong agreement.

Data Analysis

I begin by plotting the distributions across each data variable presented above, starting with the concrete data variables.

As seen above, the age and experience of the employees in this dataset is skewed right, with most employees being under 32 years old and with less than 4 years experience. The Retail Industry and HR Profession are also largely overrepresented in this dataset. Given this result, we know immediately that young employees in Retail and/or the HR profession are the optimal audience to use the machine learning model on. Also, according to the BLS, the median age of the US workforce in 2021 was about 41 years, which our dataset does have significant representation of, though not the highest. Moving forward, I now plot the distributions of the abstract data features.

These distributions are all approximately normal, as one could expect. However, because these data are self-reported and purely abstract, the integrity of this data is highly questionable. Employees can report whatever scores they want, even when they know the score is opposite to their personality. In production, I'd advise the production of two models with one model not using these abstract data as it would be more reliable and scalable.
