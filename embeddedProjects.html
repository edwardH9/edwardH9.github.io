NASA Project: Exploratory Drone/Rover Hybrid Vehicle

I lead a team of 11 students in the development of a vehicle suited to explore extreme environments. The machine comes in two variants. One is designed for recovering ground samples from the area, and uses a ground penetrating radar to locate liquid water deposits below the surface. The other, shown above, is designed for analyzing samples for bioluminescent activity using a Luciferase Assay and a luminometer. Both of these versions have the general purpose of finding life on the Moon for NASA's Artemis Program.

The machine uses Espressif microprocessors, wireless radio communication, I2C peripheral devices, H-Bridge motor drivers, and a charging system based on thermoelectric and solar power generators.
